Abstract

We propose PCHands, a novel postural synergies extraction approach for a wide range of manipulators based on anchors. We introduce Anchor Description Format (ADF) as a simplified and unified description format for manipulators ranging from 2-finger grippers to 5-finger anthropomorphic hands.

By analysing the positions of anchors in different finger configurations, PCHands is able to extract compact variable-length representation of the pose using a combination of Conditional Variational Auto-Encoder (CVAE) and Principal Component Analysis (PCA).

We show that the extracted principal components are universal across manipulators of different structure and degrees of freedom. We use PCHands for encoding state and motor space within control policies for dexterous manipulation tasks learned with Reinforcement Learning (RL), and show that PCHands outperforms the baseline in terms of learning efficiency. With ADF, we show that PCHands is robust in RL with demonstration tasks when demonstrations are provided with different manipulators.
